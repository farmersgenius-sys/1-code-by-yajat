Soil Test Guide

Step-by-step guide to test and improve your soil quality

1

Sample Collection

Collect soil samples from different parts of your field at 6-8 inch depth. Mix samples thoroughly and take 500g for testing.

2

Laboratory Testing

Take samples to nearest soil testing laboratory or use soil health card services. Tests include pH, organic carbon, NPK levels.

3

Report Analysis

Understand your soil health report. Check pH levels (6.5-7.5 ideal), nutrient status, and organic matter content.

4

Soil Improvement

Apply recommendations: lime for acidic soil, organic manure for nutrient deficiency, and appropriate fertilizers as suggested.

5

Regular Monitoring

Test soil annually before major planting season. Monitor changes and adjust farming practices accordingly for optimal crop yield.
